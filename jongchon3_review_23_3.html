FULL PASSAGE
Living things are not confined to the familiar temperate realm — they inhabit ocean depths, volcanic mud, Antarctic pools, and deep Earth's crust.
① They are unfamiliar — both inaccessible and mostly microscopic.
② Even in familiar habitats, most organisms are too small to see without equipment — going unnoticed.
③ Studies of bacteria have helped us know industrially important species used in breaking down waste. ← 흐름 무관
④ Yet microorganisms make up most of the total mass of living matter on Earth.
⑤ Only through molecular analysis and DNA sequences have we begun to see life on Earth without bias.
