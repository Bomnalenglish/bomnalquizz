FULL PASSAGE
In the Physics, Aristotle asks whether nature acts for the sake of something.
He offers an account of rain, but addresses a more general question: how to account for natural objects, events, and processes that appear with regularity.
He queries whether the sharpness of front teeth and the bluntness of molars is coincidental — and rejects that possibility.
This arrangement occurs with regularity, and that regularity is linked to purposefulness.
The sharpness of front teeth allows them to tear; the bluntness in the back enables them to crush food — both useful for eating.
In Aristotle's view, all natural things happen in a given way and occur for the sake of something — that is the final cause.
