FULL PASSAGE
Since motion is relative, only relative velocities are relevant — all velocities are relative.
On earth, we reckon velocity with respect to the ground; the earth's own velocity does not matter.
A French WWII pilot caught a bullet with his bare hand — the bullet's velocity matched the plane's velocity.
A bullet slows down due to air drag toward the end of its flight path.
Since only relative velocity matters, the bullet's momentum was negligible to the pilot — easy to catch.

💬 땅 위에서 달리는 기차 속도를 지면 기준으로 재는 것처럼 — 지구 자체 속도는 고려 안 해도 돼.
