FULL PASSAGE
Because belief is so crucial in the placebo effect, it would seem that deception is necessary to trigger a patient's trust.
① But Ted Kaptchuk is investigating the possibility of doing away with deception.
② He tells patients up front they will take a placebo, but also informs them how powerful and effective placebos can be.
③ In one trial, roughly 59% on open placebo reported improvements vs. 35% of the untreated control group.
④ If patients discover they were given a placebo, it could damage their trust in healthcare providers. ← 흐름 무관
⑤ The results may seem modest, but giving a placebo isn't the same as doing nothing.

② 믿음이 위약 효과의 핵심인데, 속임 없이는 믿음을 만들기 어렵다고 여겨지기 때문에
③ 의사가 환자에게 거짓말할 의무가 있기 때문에
④ 속임이 없으면 의학 윤리 기준에 맞지 않기 때문에
