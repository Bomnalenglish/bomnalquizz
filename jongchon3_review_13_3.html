FULL PASSAGE · 03
Becoming aware of our internal sensations can help us handle our own emotions.
The body's interoceptive faculty can also bring us into closer contact with other people's emotions.
The brain has no direct access to other people's minds — interpreting words and expressions yields only a coolly abstract sense of emotions.
The body acts as a critical conduit, supplying the brain with the visceral information it lacks.
When interacting with others, we unconsciously mimic their expressions, gestures, posture, and vocal pitch.
Via interoception of our own body's signals, we perceive what the other person feels — because we feel it in ourselves.
We bring other people's feelings onboard; the body is the bridge — we are sampling their emotions.
