주어진 문장
Subjective reality is acquired and shared through agreed-upon symbols, especially language.
Symbols are instrumental in helping people derive meanings from social situations.
( 1 ) In social encounters, each person's interpretation or definition of a given situation becomes a subjective reality from that person's viewpoint.
( 2 ) We often assume that what we consider to be "reality" is shared by others; however, this assumption is often incorrect.
( 3 ) If a person shouts "Fire!" in a crowded movie theater, for example, that language produces the same response (attempting to escape) in all of those who hear and understand it. ← 정답 위치
( 4 ) When people in a group do not share the same meaning for a given symbol, however, confusion results; for example, people who did not know the meaning of the word fire would not know what the commotion was about.
( 5 ) How people interpret the messages they receive and the situations they encounter becomes their subjective reality and may strongly influence their behavior.
