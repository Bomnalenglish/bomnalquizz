FULL PASSAGE
Hipparchus of Nicaea — the most important astronomer of the Hellenistic Age after Aristarchus.
Unlike Aristarchus, Hipparchus believed in the geocentric universe of Aristotle.
He gauged sun and moon movement using a model devised by Apollonius of Perga.
At Rhodes, he created an astronomical research center and cataloged stars by brightness.
He produced a chart of ~850 stars, tracking movements and relative brightness.
He made more accurate measurements than Aristarchus of moon/sun distances.
He developed the idea of the eccentric, which Ptolemy would later expand upon. ← ⑤번 선지 오답 근거
